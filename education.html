Introduction to Welding

  • Welding is the process of joining materials, usually metals or thermoplastics.
  • It works by causing coalescence (fusion).
  • It is distinct from soldering and brazing, which do not melt the base metal.
  • Essential in construction, automotive, and aerospace industries.

Common Welding Types

MIG (GMAW)

Ideal for beginners. Uses a fed wire. Good for manufacturing.

TIG (GTAW)

High precision. Uses tungsten electrode. Best for thin metals.

Stick (SMAW)

Versatile and rugged. Works outdoors and on dirty metal.

Safety First!

Welding hazards include electric shock, fumes, and intense UV radiation.

Essential PPE:

  • Auto-darkening Helmet (Shade 10-13)
  • Flame-resistant jacket
  • Leather gloves
  • Ventilation / Respirator

Conclusion

Welding is a skill that takes minutes to learn but a lifetime to master. Practice is key.
